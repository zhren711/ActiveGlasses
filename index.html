The Hard Problem

Powerful AI.
Tiny glasses.
All-day battery.

These three requirements have never coexisted — until now. Running a real-time vision model on a device that weighs under 50 grams and lasts all day is one of the most demanding challenges in mobile computing.

⚡
Sub-watt power budget
A glasses frame holds a 150–300 mAh battery. Every milliwatt counts. Traditional inference hardware burns 100× too much.
⏱
Strict real-time latency
Highlight detection, cooking guidance, and musical cues must respond in under 50 ms or the experience breaks entirely.
🔒
On-device privacy
First-person video is among the most sensitive data imaginable. Processing it locally — not in the cloud — is non-negotiable.

Approach 01 — FPGA

More powerful
than ever.

FPGAs let us engrave computation directly into silicon logic — not simulate it in software. For first-person capture and real-time highlight detection, this matters. When milliseconds define the experience, we build the hardware around the task.

  • Custom CNN dataflow architecture — MAC utilization far exceeds any off-the-shelf MCU solution
  • Native MIPI CSI-2: camera pixels flow directly to compute logic with zero bridging overhead
  • Fine-grained clock gating + duty-cycle control compresses average power to under 30 mW at 5% duty cycle
  • Fully reconfigurable — iterate on model architecture without changing a single piece of hardware

Approach 02 — ASIC

Unimaginably
low power.

The FPGA proves the architecture. The ASIC perfects it. Strip away the entire programmable fabric — keep only the computation. This is how you make a vision model run all day on a battery smaller than a thumbnail.

  • Eliminate FPGA routing overhead — identical workload at 10–30× lower energy per inference
  • Quantization-aware training co-designed with the hardware — INT4 with <1% accuracy loss
  • Complete RTL-to-GDSII validation via Synopsys Design Compiler, ready to inform tape-out decisions
  • Direct benchmark against GAP9 and MAX78000, with clear quantitative efficiency claims

Approach 03 — Split Computing

Fits into
your world.

The most powerful computer near your glasses is already in your pocket. Our split computing framework makes glasses and phone function as a single unified inference engine — the glasses decide, in real time, what to compute locally and what to delegate.

  • Runtime task scheduler senses complexity, battery state, and link quality — then allocates computation optimally
  • Dual-mode: BLE 5.3 for low-latency triggers, Wi-Fi 6 for high-bandwidth model offload
  • Glasses retain a featherweight always-on model; phone handles heavy lifting on demand
  • Zero extra hardware cost — leverages the GPU already in every modern smartphone
